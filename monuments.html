Monuments of India

Marvel at the Architectural Wonders Across the Ages

Iconic Monuments to Explore

Taj Mahal

Built by Emperor Shah Jahan, this white-marble mausoleum in Agra epitomizes Mughal architecture and love.

Red Fort

Constructed by Shah Jahan in Delhi, this massive fort complex served as the Mughal capital and is a UNESCO site.

Qutub Minar

At 73m, this 12th‑century minaret in Delhi is the tallest brick minaret in the world, showcasing early Indo-Islamic design.

Hampi

The ruins of Vijayanagara in Karnataka, Hampi features spectacular temple complexes and rock‑cut architecture.

Sanchi Stupa

Commissioned by Emperor Ashoka, this ancient Buddhist stupa in Madhya Pradesh is famed for its ornate gateways.

Ajanta & Ellora

These cave temples in Maharashtra date from the 2nd century BCE to 10th century CE, showcasing rock‑cut artistry.
